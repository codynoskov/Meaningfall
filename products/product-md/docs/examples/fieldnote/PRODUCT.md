# Fieldnote

## Overview

Fieldnote helps small product teams preserve interview notes, research evidence, and reusable findings without turning every discovery effort into a heavy knowledge-management program.

This ProductMD is intentionally source-backed and profile-focused. The current source material establishes identity, audience, vocabulary, and uncertainty, but it does not yet justify a durable map, action list, state model, or area model.

### Non-Goals

This file does not define research object schemas, note templates, tagging taxonomies, AI summarization behavior, permission matrices, or integration endpoints. Those may be generated or specified later after the source material is reviewed.

## Profile

Fieldnote should feel like a practical team memory product, not a research publishing platform, CRM, or general note-taking app.

### Identity

The small-team audience should keep examples and future workflows team-sized: shared interviews, lightweight synthesis, and reusable findings matter more than enterprise governance. Public attribution, support promises, sales handoff, and provider accountability should stay provisional until the `TBD` values are confirmed.

### References

The product brief should set the product vocabulary and first-version priorities. Customer notes should shape pain points and examples, but they should not be quoted publicly unless later product work confirms permission and placement.

The references are input sources for product understanding, not future public items. A later map should include research, library, team, or account items only after the product owner confirms that those are real product surfaces.

### Uncertainty

The missing provider and contact values are intentional review pressure. Agents should not fill them from generic SaaS assumptions or invent a company identity.
